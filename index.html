Bergh checking on the fermentation status of his gin.

After it’s fermented, he distills the alcohol, ages it and then flavors it with infused fruits.
4/ 11
Inspired by the outdoors and the wild herbs of the world, Bergh specializes in rums and gins that are infused with native California ingredients. Some of Bergh’s go-to flavors include oranges, peaches and apples, unique to the exemplary Big Sur coastline.
5/ 11
When asked how Bergh learned how to create spirits, he credits Cal Poly for supplying him with the opportunity to learn about the distillation process, meanwhile allowing him to be fearless in his entrepreneurial pursuits.
6 / 11
Bergh and his colleague bottling spirits to be sold locally to customers. The spirits can be found in retail stores through California such as Bevmo, Vons, Bristol Farms, Whole Foods and California Fresh Market.
7 / 11
The labeling reflects Bergh’s overall branding and “vibes” of the distillery. The labels portray illustrations of California outdoors and wildlife, and are inspired by local scenery.
8 / 11
Bergh’s 6,000-square-foot distillery and tasting room. The visitor area features a redwood bar along with copper tables for guests. The tasting room is situated in Paso Robles Wine Country, close in proximity to Tin City.
9 / 11
One of the youngest distillers in the nation, Bergh’s slogan is “Liquid California”, in which he yearns for creativity and experimentation in his masterpieces.
10 / 11
Calwise’s signature drink is the “Big Sur Gin” which is infused with 12 organic herbs that are native to the renowned Big Sur coastline, including that of sage, bay, fennel, and elderberry aromas. The bottle is distilled locally.
11 / 11
Bergh’s Big Sur Gin has already earned a Best of Show medal at the 2017 Wine & Spirits Wholesalers of America’s Annual Convention and Exposition. He has also been awarded a gold medal at the 2017 Los Angeles International Wine and Spirits Competition.
